Three Bogatyrs

Ilya

Ilya Muromets is one of the bogatyrs (epic knights) in Bylinas of Kievan Rus. The tales are set in the time of the Kievan Rus'. Attempts have been made to identify a possible historical nucleus for the character. The main candidate is Ilya Pechersky, a monk of the 12th century who was beatified in the Orthodox Church in 1643. His relics are preserved in the Kiev Monastery of the Caves

Dobrynya

Dobrynya Nikitich is one of the most popular bogatyrs (epic knights) from Russian folklore. Albeit fictional, this character is based on a real warlord Dobrynya, who led the armies of Svyatoslav the Great and tutored his son Vladimir the Great. Many Byliny center on Dobrynya completing tasks set him by prince Vladimir. As a courtier, Dobrynya seems to be a representative of the noble class of warriors.

Alyosha

Alyosha Popovich is a folk hero in the Rus' folklore, a bogatyr (epic knight). He is the youngest of the three main bogatyrs. In Byliny (ballads) he is described as a clever-minded priest's son who wins by tricking and outsmarting his foes. He defeated the dragon Tugarin Zmeyevich by trickery.
